Preparing Your RV for a Trip to Alaska

The Alaska Highway is the adventure of a lifetime — but it demands serious preparation.

⚠️ The Alaska Highway (ALCAN) has significant stretches of gravel, frost heaves, and construction. Plan for rock chips on windshields and potential tire damage. Many RVers get a windshield chip within the first 200 miles.
Vehicle & Mechanical

🔧 Complete Service Before You Go

Oil change, transmission fluid, coolant flush, brake inspection, and wheel bearing repack. The nearest RV service center in Alaska could be hundreds of miles away. Arrive in perfect mechanical condition.

🛡 Windshield Protection

Install a quality windshield protector film or carry RV-specific windshield covers. Gravel roads and logging trucks kick up rocks constantly. Budget for one windshield replacement — it's almost expected on the ALCAN.

🔩 Carry Extra Spare Parts

Extra belts, hoses, filters, fuses, a spare tire (or two), tire repair kit, and basic tools. Auto parts stores are scarce. Carry everything you'd need for minor repairs on the road.

⛽ Fuel Planning

Never pass a gas station without topping off. Stations can be 100+ miles apart. Fuel is $1-2 more per gallon than the US. Budget accordingly — a Class A can easily spend $3,000+ in fuel one-way.

Living Systems

❄️ Cold Weather Prep

Even in summer, Alaska nights drop below freezing. Ensure your furnace works, all water lines are insulated, and you have adequate propane. The interior temperature can swing 40°F in a single day.

🦟 Mosquito Defense

Alaska mosquitoes are legendary. Carry serious DEET-based repellent, screen door patches, and consider a screen room for your awning area. This is not optional — some areas are genuinely unbearable without protection.

🐻 Bear Safety

Store all food in hard-sided containers or your RV. Never leave food outside unattended. Carry bear spray and know how to use it. Research specific bear protocols for each area you visit — black bear and grizzly require different responses.

Planning & Logistics

📡 Communication

Cell service disappears for hundreds of miles at a time. Invest in a satellite communicator (Garmin inReach or SPOT) for emergency communication. Download offline maps for all of Alaska and the ALCAN before you leave.

🏕 Campground Reservations

Book Denali, Kenai Fjords, and other popular spots months in advance. Many dispersed camping areas are first-come-first-served but popular spots fill early. Dump stations are sparse — plan your black tank strategy carefully.

🛂 Entry Requirements

You must pass through Canada to reach Alaska by road. Ensure passports are current for all travelers. Firearms have specific Canadian entry requirements — research this thoroughly before crossing. Pets need health certificates.

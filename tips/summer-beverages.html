🥤 TIP

Best Refreshing Non-Alcoholic Beverages for Summer RVing

Stay hydrated and cool at the campsite — no cooler full of alcohol required.

🍋

Campsite Lemonade

The classic. Easy to make in bulk, keeps well in a jug, and everyone loves it. Add fresh mint or sliced strawberries for a campsite upgrade.

Recipe: 1 cup fresh lemon juice · 1 cup simple syrup · 4 cups cold water · Ice · Optional: fresh mint or strawberries
🍵

Sun Tea

One of the great RV traditions. Fill a gallon jar with water and 8 tea bags, set it in the sun for 3-4 hours, and you have perfectly brewed sweet tea with zero effort. Add peach slices for a Southern upgrade.

Recipe: 1 gallon water · 8 black tea bags · 4 hours in direct sun · Sweeten to taste · Serve over ice
🌿

Agua Fresca

A Mexican campsite staple — refreshing, light, and infinitely customizable. Watermelon is the most popular version but cucumber-lime is a close second for hot days.

Watermelon version: 4 cups cubed watermelon · 2 cups water · Juice of 2 limes · 2 tbsp sugar · Blend, strain, serve over ice
🫚

Sparkling Water Bar

Pack a 12-pack of plain sparkling water and a bag of fresh fruit and herbs. Set it up as a self-serve "flavor bar" — sliced citrus, cucumber, mint, basil, berries. Kids love it and it keeps everyone hydrated without sugar overload.

Must-haves: Lemon · Lime · Orange · Cucumber · Fresh mint · Frozen berries as ice cubes
🥥

Coconut Water Cooler

Coconut water with lime and a splash of pineapple juice over ice. Naturally hydrating with electrolytes — perfect for hot days when you've been outside all day. Better than sports drinks and no artificial ingredients.

Recipe: 8 oz coconut water · Juice of 1 lime · 2 oz pineapple juice · Ice · Mint garnish optional
🍹

Virgin Campfire Mule

All the satisfaction of a Moscow Mule without the alcohol. Ginger beer has a kick that feels like a grown-up drink. Great for the campfire circle when everyone else has a cocktail.

Recipe: 6 oz ginger beer · Juice of 1 lime · 2 oz cranberry or pomegranate juice · Ice · Lime wedge garnish
☕

Cold Brew Coffee

Make it the night before — coarsely ground coffee steeped in cold water for 12 hours in the fridge. Smooth, low-acid, and keeps you going on long driving days. Add a splash of cream and a pinch of cinnamon for a campsite treat.

Recipe: 1 cup coarse ground coffee · 4 cups cold water · Steep 12 hours in fridge · Strain through coffee filter · Serve over ice
🫐

Berry Shrub

A drinking vinegar shrub sounds unusual but is incredibly refreshing on a hot day. Mix berries, sugar, and apple cider vinegar, let sit 2 days, and add a splash to sparkling water. Complex, tangy, and surprisingly addictive.

Recipe: 1 cup mashed berries · 1 cup sugar · 1 cup apple cider vinegar · Sit 2 days · Strain · Add 2 tbsp to sparkling water over ice

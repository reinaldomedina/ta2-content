How to Hitch a Travel Trailer Safely

⚠️ Safety Disclaimer: Always consult your tow vehicle and trailer owner's manuals for specific requirements. When in doubt, have a certified RV technician inspect your setup. Improper hitching can result in serious accidents.
1

Check Your Tow Ratings

Before anything else, confirm your tow vehicle's Gross Combined Weight Rating (GCWR), tow capacity, and tongue weight limit. Never exceed these ratings — your trailer's loaded weight must stay within all three limits.

2

Position the Tow Vehicle

Back the tow vehicle so the ball is directly under the trailer coupler. Use a spotter if available. The trailer tongue should be slightly higher than the ball to allow the coupler to drop onto it cleanly.

3

Lower the Coupler onto the Ball

Lower the tongue jack until the coupler sits fully on the ball. The coupler latch should snap closed. Pull up on the trailer tongue to confirm it's locked — it should not come off. Insert the safety pin or clip through the coupler latch.

4

Attach Safety Chains

Cross the safety chains under the tongue in an X pattern. Leave enough slack to allow turns but not so much that they drag. Chains are your backup if the coupler fails — they must be rated for your trailer's weight.

5

Connect the Breakaway Cable

Attach the breakaway cable to the tow vehicle — NOT to the safety chain mount. This cable activates the trailer brakes if the trailer separates. Loop it through a fixed point so it pulls taut only if the trailer breaks away.

6

Plug in the Electrical Connector

Connect the 7-pin (or 4-pin) connector. Test all lights — running lights, brake lights, turn signals, and reverse lights. Never tow without working trailer lights.

7

Raise the Tongue Jack

Fully retract the tongue jack so it clears the ground. Secure it in the travel position. A dragging jack can cause catastrophic loss of control.

8

Install Weight Distribution & Sway Control

If your setup requires a weight distribution hitch or sway control bar, install them now per manufacturer instructions. These are critical for trailers over 50% of the tow vehicle's weight.

9

Final Walk-Around

Walk completely around the rig: confirm coupler is locked, chains are crossed and attached, breakaway cable is connected, jack is up, all lights work, and nothing is dragging or hanging. Check trailer tires and wheel chocks are removed.

🚐 Pro Tip After your first mile, pull over and re-check the coupler, chains, and weight distribution bars. Movement during the first stretch can reveal issues that weren't visible at the campsite.

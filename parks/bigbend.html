Big Bend National Park

Updated May 2026 · 8 min read

Big Bend is one of the most remote and rewarding national parks in the United States. Sitting along the Rio Grande in southwest Texas, it offers dramatic desert landscapes, mountain ranges, and some of the darkest skies in the country.

🚐 RV Tip: The roads to Chisos Basin can be challenging for larger rigs. Class A motorhomes over 24 feet and trailers over 20 feet should use the Rio Grande Village campground instead.

Campgrounds for RVers

Big Bend has three main campgrounds: Chisos Basin, Rio Grande Village, and Cottonwood. Rio Grande Village is the most RV-friendly with hookups available. Reservations are strongly recommended from October through April.

What to Do

The Lost Mine Trail offers stunning panoramic views and is accessible to most visitors. The hot springs near Rio Grande Village are a must — a short walk leads to natural thermal pools right on the river bank.

Getting There

The nearest town is Marathon, TX — about 40 miles north. Stock up on supplies before entering the park. Cell service is essentially nonexistent inside the park boundaries, so download offline maps before you arrive.
